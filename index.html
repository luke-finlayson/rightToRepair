The Right to Repair

So, Whats the deal?

A few decades ago, if something you had broke, you could just fix it or get your neighbor to do it for you. If none of those were options, then you could find some local repair shop and they would fix it for you at a cheap price.

These days, those options are quickly disapearing, and its becoming harder to repair electronic equiptment, such as computers, phones and cars.

Unfortunatly, this is the result of planned obsolencence. Maunfactures are deliberatly designing their products to only last a few years at most. They refuse to publish any sort of repair documentation, suggesting that if your product breaks, you take it to one of their authorised 'repair' shops. The 'Right to Repair' movement was created to stop this from continuing. The people involved in the movement have been attempting to pass new legistation laws for several years now, however large companys, such as Apple, have been successfully blocking them.

Ok, but why do we have to worry?

Globally, 50 million tons of electronic waste is generated every year, thats the weight of over 8000 elephants! Repairing electonic products gets them back into the hands of people who can keep using them. It keeps the rubbish out of the landfills and reduces the increasing problem of pollution.
